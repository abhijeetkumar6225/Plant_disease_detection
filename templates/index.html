About the project

How AI reads a leaf

Leaflet uses a Convolutional Neural Network trained on the PlantVillage dataset — over 87,000 images across 38 disease categories and 14 plant species. The model learns visual patterns in leaf texture, color gradients, and lesion geometry that the human eye easily misses.

Each image goes through preprocessing, feature extraction across multiple convolutional layers, and a final softmax classifier that returns a probability distribution across all 38 classes. The highest-probability class and its confidence are surfaced to you, along with agronomic information to act on.

The process

From leaf to diagnosis in 5 steps

📷

Upload leaf photo

Drag & drop or browse a JPG/PNG image of any leaf. Close-up shots in natural light work best.

⚙️

AI preprocessing

The image is resized to 128×128, normalized to [0,1], and passed into the model pipeline.

🧠

Deep learning inference

The CNN extracts visual features — texture, color gradients, lesion patterns — through its convolutional layers.

📊

Disease prediction

A softmax classifier assigns probabilities across all 38 classes. The highest-confidence class is selected.

💊

Treatment recommendations

The predicted class is matched to a curated knowledge base with symptoms, treatment, and prevention advice.
